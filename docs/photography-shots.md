# Photography Shot List

Site-type-specific photography guidance. Each shot has a priority, label, description, and rationale. The parser extracts shots grouped by type.

## Universal

Every site needs these regardless of business type.

- **Hero image** — priority: must-have — Wide, well-lit, uncluttered. Represents what you do at a glance. — This is the first thing visitors see. A strong hero image sets the tone for the entire site.
- **Owner / team portrait** — priority: must-have — Natural light, relaxed pose. People buy from people. — Visitors trust businesses with real faces. Skip the corporate headshot — be approachable.
- **Work in progress** — priority: high-value — You doing the thing — not posing, actually working. — Action shots build credibility and show your craft in motion.
- **Detail / texture shot** — priority: high-value — Close-up of your craft, product, or environment. — Detail shots add visual richness and break up text-heavy pages.
- **Space / location** — priority: nice-to-have — Where you work, if it matters to the brand. — Helps visitors picture themselves in your space before they arrive.

## Restaurant

Covers: restaurant, cafe, coffee-shop, bakery, catering, deli, ice-cream, juice-bar, food-truck, home-baker, cottage-food

- **Signature dish** — priority: must-have — Hero plating shot from overhead and 45-degree angle. Best dish, best plate, best light. — Food is your product. One stunning dish photo is worth more than ten average ones.
- **Dining room or bar** — priority: high-value — Empty and inviting, morning light preferred. Show the ambiance. — Visitors deciding where to eat want to see the vibe before they book.
- **Kitchen action** — priority: high-value — Chef or cook at work — steam, motion, focus. Not posed. — Behind-the-scenes energy signals quality and passion.
- **Menu board or specials** — priority: nice-to-have — Chalkboard, printed menu, or display case. Legible and well-lit. — Adds authenticity and shows you update your offerings.
- **Exterior and signage** — priority: nice-to-have — Entrance, sign, outdoor seating. Helps people find you. — The "is this the place?" photo. Critical for first-time visitors navigating to you.

## Retail

Covers: retail, shop, boutique, gift-shop, bookstore, pet-store, hardware, garden-center

- **Product flatlay** — priority: must-have — 3-5 items on a consistent background. Top-down, even lighting. — Clean product photography builds trust and makes items feel buyable online.
- **Product in use** — priority: high-value — Lifestyle context — someone wearing, using, or enjoying the product. — Helps customers picture themselves with the item. Lifestyle beats catalog.
- **Storefront exterior** — priority: high-value — Clean shot of the entrance and signage during business hours. — The digital equivalent of curb appeal — makes you look established and welcoming.
- **Inside the shop** — priority: nice-to-have — Wide angle showing breadth of selection, well-organized. — Conveys the experience of browsing in-person.
- **Packaging detail** — priority: nice-to-have — Close-up of distinctive packaging, tags, or branding. — If your packaging is part of the brand, show it off.

## Service

Covers: contractor, cleaner, landscaper, plumber, electrician, painter, roofer, hvac, handyman, pest-control, moving, auto-repair, mechanic

- **Before and after** — priority: must-have — Minimum two jobs. Same angle, same framing. The transformation tells the story. — Nothing sells service work like visible proof of results.
- **Team on-site** — priority: must-have — Crew working, gear and uniforms visible. Real job site, not staged. — Shows you're professional, equipped, and ready to work.
- **Finished work close-up** — priority: high-value — Detail of craftsmanship — clean lines, quality materials, precision. — Close-ups prove quality in ways wide shots can't.
- **Vehicle or equipment** — priority: nice-to-have — Branded truck, specialized tools, or equipment if customer-facing. — Professional equipment signals reliability and investment in the business.
- **Satisfied customer** — priority: nice-to-have — With permission. Candid preferred over posed — a handshake, a smile at the result. — Social proof in photo form. More powerful than a written testimonial.

## Professional

Covers: consultant, therapist, accountant, lawyer, financial-advisor, insurance, real-estate, coach, tutor, counselor

- **Professional portrait** — priority: must-have — Office or neutral background. Approachable, not stiff. Natural light from a window. — Your face is your brand. Clients hire people they feel comfortable with.
- **Workspace** — priority: high-value — Desk, bookshelf, whatever signals expertise without clutter. — Shows you have a real practice and take your work seriously.
- **Working shot** — priority: high-value — At the laptop, in a meeting, at a whiteboard. Not looking at the camera. — Candid working shots feel more authentic than posed portraits.
- **Physical deliverable** — priority: nice-to-have — Report, materials, workbook, or any tangible output of your service. — Makes abstract services feel concrete and valuable.

## Portfolio

Covers: photographer, designer, artist, illustrator, videographer, musician, writer, architect, interior-designer, web-designer

- **Best work samples** — priority: must-have — 6-10 strongest pieces, consistent aspect ratio. Quality over quantity. — Your portfolio IS your product. Curate ruthlessly.
- **Process shot** — priority: high-value — Mid-creation. Sketch, edit screen, raw materials, work in progress. — Shows the human behind the work and builds narrative around your craft.
- **Studio or workspace** — priority: high-value — Where the magic happens. Clean but lived-in. — Adds personality and context that a portfolio grid alone can't convey.
- **Tools of the trade** — priority: nice-to-have — Close-up of camera gear, brushes, instruments, software. — Detail shots of tools signal expertise and investment in craft.

## Nonprofit

Covers: nonprofit, charity, church, community, school, youth-program, animal-rescue, food-bank, shelter

- **People being served** — priority: must-have — With permission and dignity. Show impact, not pity. Faces, not crowds. — Donors give to people, not organizations. Show who benefits.
- **Volunteers at work** — priority: must-have — Hands-on, engaged, purposeful. Show the community in action. — Volunteer photos recruit more volunteers and show donors their money at work.
- **Event coverage** — priority: high-value — Energy over perfection. Capture the crowd, the activity, the emotion. — Events are proof of life. They show momentum and community engagement.
- **Impact evidence** — priority: nice-to-have — Before/after, outcome stats on display, thank-you notes, graduation photos. — Concrete evidence of results turns interest into donations.

## Fitness

Covers: gym, yoga, pilates, martial-arts, personal-trainer, dance-studio, crossfit, swimming

- **Facility and equipment** — priority: must-have — Clean, well-lit space. Show the best area. Morning light, no people for the main shot. — People want to see where they'll be working out before committing.
- **Class in session** — priority: must-have — Instructor leading, participants engaged. Energy and movement. — Shows the experience, not just the space. Captures the vibe.
- **Trainer or instructor portrait** — priority: high-value — Active but approachable. In workout attire, in the space. — The instructor is often the reason someone picks a studio.
- **Equipment detail** — priority: nice-to-have — Specialty equipment, clean and organized. — Signals investment and variety for equipment-conscious members.

## Salon

Covers: salon, barbershop, spa, nail-salon, tattoo, piercing, medspa, esthetician

- **Finished work** — priority: must-have — Best haircuts, colors, nails, tattoos. Consistent lighting and backdrop. — Your work IS your portfolio. One incredible result photo beats ten okay ones.
- **Station or chair** — priority: high-value — Clean, styled, inviting. The space where the service happens. — Clients want to see where they'll be sitting for an hour or more.
- **Stylist at work** — priority: high-value — Hands in hair, focused on craft. Mid-service, not posed. — Shows skill and care in action. Builds confidence in the provider.
- **Products used** — priority: nice-to-have — Product shelf or featured brands. Organized, well-lit. — Product lines signal quality tier and can drive retail add-ons.
